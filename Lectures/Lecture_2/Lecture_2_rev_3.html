3. Trigonometric Functions

Trigonometric functions are essential for describing periodic phenomena like waves and rotations. They are defined using the unit circleâ€”a circle with a radius of 1 centered at the origin.

For any angle $\theta$, the point $(x,y)$ where the angle's terminal side intersects the unit circle gives us $x = \cos(\theta)$ and $y = \sin(\theta)$.
